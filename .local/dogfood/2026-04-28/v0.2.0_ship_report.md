### The T2_pass_k blocker is structural, not effort-related

The deterministic SHA-256 simulator emits `pass_k_4 = pass_rate^4` as
a documented PROXY — a lower bound. Real k=4 sampling against an LLM
typically produces real `pass_k_4 > mean^k` because variance pushes
some runs above the mean. This is why Round 12's "Option A" attempt
(adding a new case to lift T2_pass_k) cannot work: the simulator's
proxy is structurally constrained by the per-case mean, and adding
more cases just averages-in their proxy values.

The genuine unblock is **a real-LLM runner** (`evals/si-chip/runners/real_llm_runner.py`)
that does k=4 independent sampling per prompt against composer_2 / sonnet
/ opus. This is the v0.3.0 PRIMARY MILESTONE (see Round 13
`next_action_plan.yaml a1`).

### Why v0.2.0 ships at v1_baseline anyway

Spec §4.2 promotion rule has TWO clauses:

1. The current gate must be satisfied (Si-Chip is currently bound to
   `v1_baseline`).
2. To PROMOTE to the next gate, two consecutive rounds must clear every
   threshold of that next gate.

Si-Chip satisfies clause 1 trivially: 13 consecutive v1_baseline passes.
Si-Chip ships v0.2.0 at the **current** gate (v1_baseline = relaxed),
which is the SAME gate as the v0.1.0 ship. The master plan
ASPIRATIONALLY targeted v2_tightened for v0.2.0 but did not make it
Normative-mandated; v0.2.0 at v1_baseline is consistent with:

* Spec §8.3 multi-round requirement (13 consecutive v1_baseline passes).
